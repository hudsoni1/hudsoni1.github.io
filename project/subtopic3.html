AI-Patient Relationships

URL Physician persepctives on the impact of artificial intelligence on the therapeutic relationship in mental health care: Qualitative study
Author Isabel B Weir et al.
Abstract This article looks at how the inclusion of AI into therapy or clinical psychology can impact the therapeutic relationship between the patient and the provider. Amongst the interview research conducted, several physicians acknowledged that AI tools can impact the relationship in many ways. There can still be an established relationship with the AI service and the patient (referred to as an AI-Patient relationship), and this can greatly reduce miscommunication. However, the authors recognize that many reports showed concerns about how AI may not be able to empathize with the patient; which is crucial to mental health care. Overall, clinical relationships become strained and threatened in the age of AI integration.

URL Experts caution against using AI chatbots for emotional support
Author Sherri Gardner
Abstract As AI chatbots are becoming more popular in psychological care -- specifically mental health care -- it is important to recognize how this change can impact the people receiving the care. The article explains the tradeoffs that we often see in the field. AI can adapt to the characteristics of the human they are speaking to, mirroring their language and becoming indistinguishable from a true human. This makes the chatbot appealing to its users. However, the authors argue that AI will never be able to fully replace the impact of the human connection, nor the patient-provider relationship. While it has successes in accessibility and 24/7 support, it can also provide incorrect information at the expense of convenience. The authors highlight the positives and negatives that come from the AI-patient relationship.
