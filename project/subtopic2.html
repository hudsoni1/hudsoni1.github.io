Clinical Diagnosis in AI

URL AI diagnoses mental health disorders with high accuracy
Author Monica Vilhauer
Abstract This article looks at how AI can diagnose mental health disorders and perform clinical interviews. Although AI in psychology is used for a multitude of things, AI powered clinical interviews might be the next big innovation in the field. Research has shown that AI has been able to estimate if participants had a change of meeting DSM-5 criteria for a mental health diagnosis. AI diagnoses have been regarded as accurate and applicable, but the recency of the innovation requires more evidence of scalability and generalizability, to create a positive intervention. AI tools can provide tools to address limitations in current diagnoses from in-person providers.

URL Editorial: AI approach to the psychiatric diagnosis and prediction
Author Wenjing Gao et al.
Abstract This article focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of psychiatric disorders, and how traditional methods are now being surpassed by AI. AI has developed advanced tools, such as machine learning and natural language processing. These tools are able to identify pathology while extensively analyzing clinical interviews and screenings. While research has shown that diagnoses can be made more accurately, disease progression -- or prognosis -- and treatments responses can also be monitored. The authors also note the challenges that this innovation brings forth, such as issues of reliability and validity in the conducted research. With considering these risks, research should continue to develop and expand the extent of the decision-making process AI is capable of having.
