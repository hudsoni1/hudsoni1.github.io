AI in Mental Health Counseling

URL Transforming mental health research and care through artificial intelligence
Author Nils Opel & Michael Breakspear
Abstract The recent inclusion of AI in clinical care, specifically mental health care, has been highly questioned in its ability to provide safe mental health care for all populations. Mental health care's recent technologies utilize complex imaging, genetic, and molecular signaling data to improve therapeutic strategies and mental health diagnoses. AI holds potential to care for mental illnesses, but challenges and risks must be considered to ensure effective care. Behavioral and non-verbal assessments require the most consideration. The authors argue that the use of AI in mental health care should be regarded as an individual journey, tailored to each patient, promoting well-being.

URL Ai is changing every aspect of psychology. Here's what to watch for
Author Zara Abrams
Abstract This article looks at how AI is currently being integrated into psychology and how to adjust to the change. While AI has the potential to be an equipped technology, there are still concerns on their responses in mental health based settings; such as spreading of misinformation or discriminatory responses. Researchers and psychologists aim to reduce these potential biases so that AI-based mental health treatment can be equal and fair. It is also clear that AI in the context of clinical settings is not equatable to the nuances of a human therapist. As long as AI is inclusive, and data is protected, AI can transform mental health research, bringing forth data on human behaviors.
